01About me

I grew up in Balasore, Odisha, and what pulled me into computer science was the same thing that's always pulled me in: patterns, numbers, and good puzzles. Probability and competitive maths were my first love, and a lot of what I enjoy now — algorithms, chess, a clean line of reasoning — grows out of that.

Today I'm a security software engineer at Samsung Electronics in South Korea, working in confidential computing: operating-system hardening, trusted execution environments, and remote attestation. Underneath that I care about the fundamentals — the Linux kernel, compilers, concurrency, and the algorithms that hold it all together.

I studied Computer Science at IIT Kanpur, where my projects ranged from a Python-to-x86 compiler to kernel internals and randomized algorithms. That mix — low-level systems plus a strong theory backbone — is exactly the space I like working in.

Beyond work I stay curious — I follow competitive programming closely and have been picking up some machine learning on the side. Away from the desk you'll find me at a chessboard, on a badminton court, watching football, painting, writing, cooking, or deep in an anime.

02What I'm working on
Security Software EngineerSep 2025 — Present

vTPM-TD Security Review & Hardening

High-level security review of vTPM-TD integration with the SPAC architecture — verified the attestation chain, identified an AK-chain binding vulnerability, investigated rollback risks, and now running a code-level hardening review (side-channel resistance, RNG seeding, attack-surface reduction, secure state handling, policy enforcement).

Google Oak Session Attestation Prefetching

Customized the Oak Session attestation flow to demonstrate cached evidence injection via a custom prefetched-evidence provider; verified handshake, session binding, and encrypted exchange still complete, and that perfect forward secrecy appears preserved at the Noise layer.

aTLS & Cloud Integration Vulnerability Analysis

Reviewed attested-TLS and attestation-based session establishment for Samsung's private AI cloud — trust boundaries, evidence validation, freshness and binding assumptions, plus a review of the bastion host image in the integration path.

VigiloOS Kernel, Policy & Platform Hardening

Hardening VigiloOS, a hardened OS for confidential VMs in Samsung's private cloud — KSPP-based kernel hardening and Kconfig tuning, TDX/PCI/ACPI device-policy logic for an allowlist exposure model, a dm-crypt ephemeral read-write filesystem, and self-test coverage for platform restrictions.
